CARROLL

01 Apr 1828
CARROLL created from Non-County Area 4, from part of ADAMS New Purchase attached to HAMILTON, and from part of WABASH New Purchase attached to PUTNAM. Non-County Area 11 attached to CARROLL. Part of ADAMS New Purchase detached from HAMILTON, attached to CARROLL; and part of WABASH New Purchase detached from PUTNAM, attached to CARROLL. Non-County Area 10 was attached concurrently to CARROLL and TIPPECANOE. All attachments were for administrative and judicial purposes. (Ind. Acts 1827, 12th sess., ch. 16/p. 21 and ch. 10, secs. 3, 6/p. 14)
14 Apr 1829
CARROLL lost part of ADAMS New Purchase attachment, all of WABASH New Purchase attachment, and part of Non-County Area 11 attachment to creation of CASS. Part of Non-County Area 11 was detached from CARROLL, became part of Non-County Area 12 attached to CASS. Remaining part of ADAMS New Purchase was detached from CARROLL, attached to CASS. All attachments were for administrative and judical purposes. (Ind. Acts 1828, 13th sess., ch. 19/p. 26 and ch. 20/p. 28)
01 Apr 1830
CARROLL lost part of Non-County Area 10 (concurrently attached to CARROLL and TIPPECANOE) to creation of ST. JOSEPH, and to creation of Non-County Area 14, which was attached to ST. JOSEPH for administrative and judicial purposes. (Ind. Acts 1829, 14th sess., ch. 23, secs. 1-2, 7-9, 14/pp. 28-31)

JOSEPH; remaining Non-County Area 10 was detached from concurrent jurisdiction of CARROLL and TIPPECANOE and attached to CARROLL only. Non-County Area 11 attached to CARROLL lost all territory to Non-County Area 10; Non-County Area 11 eliminated. (Ind. Acts 1830, 15th sess., spec., ch. 12, secs. 1-2, 7-8/pp. 16-18)

CARROLL lost WABASH New Purchase attachment and part of Non-County Area 10 attachment to creation of WHITE. (Ind. Acts 1833, 18th sess., ch. 30/p. 67)

CARROLL lost to WHITE; mistake in description corrected 14 February 1839. (Ind. Acts 1836, 21st sess., loc., ch. 170/p. 379)

16 Feb 1839
Part of ADAMS New Purchase (Great Miami Reservation) was attached to CARROLL for administrative and judicial purposes. (Ind. Acts 1838, 23d sess., gen., ch. 63/p. 75)

CARROLL boundaries were redefined, creating an overlap with WHITE, but change never took effect; corrected 13 January 1846. (Ind. Rev. Stat. 1843, ch. 2, secs. 2, 26, 28-31, 36-37, 42-44, 61, 67, 77, 80-82, 85-86/pp. 68, 74-81, 84, 86, 88-89)

RICHARDVILLE (now HOWARD) fully organized, detached from CARROLL. (Ind. Acts 1843, 28th sess., gen., ch. 3, sec. 6/p. 11)
13 Jan 1845
CARROLL lost ADAMS New Purchase attachment to RICHARDVILLE (now HOWARD), mistakes in description corrected 13 January 1846. (Ind. Acts 1844, 29th sess., loc., ch. 126/p. 151)
13 Jan 1846
CARROLL boundaries were redefined, correcting mistake of 5 December 1842 and implicitly clarifying the boundary with WHITE. Boundary between CARROLL and RICHARDVILLE (now HOWARD) was redefined, correcting mistakes of 13 January 1845 [no change]. (Ind. Acts 1845, 30th sess., loc., chs. 237, 246/pp. 260, 264)
